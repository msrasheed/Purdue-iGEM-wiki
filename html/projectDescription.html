Project Description

Abstract

Systemic candidiasis--caused by the fungal pathogen Candida albicans--is the fourth leading cause of hospital-acquired infections, with a mortality rate of up to 49%. Current method of diagnosis focus on either cell culture (which is time-consuming) or xxx Timely diagnosis of candidiasis, especially in rural or underdeveloped regions, is challenging because it requires specialized equipment and expertise. Purdue iGEM is developing a paper-based, diagnostic test for C. albicans that is rapid, cost-effective, and user-friendly. The test detects the biomarkers farnesol and tyrosol by binding them to the proteins pqsR and tyrosinase respectively. Upon binding, horseradish peroxidase activates and produces an amplified colorimetric signal by oxidizing the substrate tetramethylbenzidine (TMB). Implementing our diagnostic test will provide a simple, colorimetric output, which can be interpreted by the user to take the appropriate action, i.e. administer antifungals if needed. Using such a test will control the abuse of antifungals and thus, limit the propagation of antimicrobial resistance.
